OTTER

Open Toolkit for Training in Education Research

OTTER is a graduate-student methods learning collective at The Ohio State University. Each month, one of us takes the lead on a research method — three weeks of study together, one public talk open to all. The methods we explore, and the materials we produce, live here for the community.

How It Works

How OTTER Works

OTTER runs on a monthly cycle, designed so that we learn deeply as a small group and share generously with the broader community.

1

Weeks 1–3
Group Study

The monthly leader selects a method, shares one to two readings, and guides three weeks of small-group discussion within EED.

2

Week 4
Public Talk

The leader gives a talk on the method to all OSU students — in person, with Zoom occasionally when needed. Open to all. Not recorded.

3

Ongoing
Open Materials

Slides from every public talk live on this site, with readings and notes, free for anyone to revisit or adapt.

Who joins the study

Currently open to PhD students in Engineering Education at OSU. Contact a team member to join the next cycle.

Who joins the talks

Open to all OSU students across departments. External researchers welcome by request. RSVP via the form for each upcoming talk.
